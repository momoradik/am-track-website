The Problem

What Small AM Companies Do Today

A typical 10-30 person AM shop runs on:

Excel

Job tracking, inventory counts, timesheets, BOMs, customer lists — all in disconnected spreadsheets that break when someone forgets to save.

WhatsApp

Task assignments, machine status updates, urgent requests — buried in group chats that nobody can search.

Paper Forms

Quality inspections, SOP sign-offs, visitor logs — filed in cabinets that nobody opens until an audit.

Email

Purchase orders, customer approvals, delivery tracking — scattered across inboxes with no central visibility.

Why Enterprise Software Doesn't Work

  • SAP Business One: £150/user/month + £50k implementation. A 15-person shop pays £27k/year before it's even configured.
  • Oracle NetSuite: £100/user/month + 6-12 month implementation. Requires dedicated IT staff.

  • Odoo: Free core but £500/user/year for manufacturing modules. Complex self-hosting. Not AM-specific.

Result: 80%+ of small AM companies operate with no proper digital system. They know they need one. They just can't afford it or justify the implementation overhead.

Acquirers:

Exit Option 2: Lifestyle Business (Sustainable Path)

Timeline: Indefinite

At 300 customers and £200k ARR with 73% margins, the business generates £146k/year in profit with minimal ongoing effort. AI handles development and support. This is a highly attractive lifestyle business if the founders choose not to sell.

Exit Option 3: Private Equity Roll-Up

Timeline: Year 4–6

PE firms actively acquiring vertical SaaS businesses. AMTrack's local-first model, near-zero infrastructure costs, and high retention make it an ideal roll-up target alongside complementary manufacturing software companies.

Exit Option 4: Management Buyout

Timeline: Year 3+

The customer success hire or a senior customer could acquire the business. Low operational complexity makes this feasible.

Key insight: Because our costs are so low (no cloud, no engineering team, AI-built), the business is profitable from Month 8. This means we never need to raise additional capital to reach exit readiness. The seed funding is the only external capital needed.
